### Warm Fuzzies

- Irish-ness
- Apple dumplings
- Charles leaving is very good drama
- Quakers having guns is interesting
- Religious conflict is good
- Stakes are high and good the whole time
- Dynamic of John being involved in spying and the whole family
- This family works like a democracy, which is unusual in this time period
- So many scenes ended with prayer and scriptures
- It is a family effort but Lydia impels action
- The family remains non-violent and is still useful
- Lydia gets taken by the people to see general Howe and John is ready to charge in
- Lydia isn't forced to lie
- From having been in the Quaker meeting houses and the idea of avoiding conflict and the democratic nature of the faith.
	- That desire to follow the faith is not a crusade and it is not who they are.
	- William does not preach to his children
	- Room for interpretation of scripture
	- They dress differently and have a different religion
- The family is still accepted by the community
- Barrington
- Observed the rhythmic dialog, and it was very fluid and good
